What is Neobank?

A neobank is a type of digital bank that works entirely online without any physical branches. You can open accounts, send money, pay bills, and manage your finances through a mobile app or website. Neobanks focus on making banking easy, fast, and affordable. They offer low fees, clear pricing, and smarter tools to help you manage your money. Unlike traditional banks, neobanks often provide more personalized services and are built for people who prefer modern, tech-driven banking.

Frequently asked questions

Q1. What tech is used for Neobank app development?

The Neobank app development tech stack includes React Native, cloud services, Microsoft Azure, Ruby on Rails, and many more of the latest technologies.

Q2. How Much Does It Cost to Develop a Neobank App?

The cost to develop a neobank app typically ranges from $30,000 to $265,000+, depending on the app's complexity, features, and the location of the development team.

  • Basic apps – $40,000–$60,000
  • Advanced apps – $100,000–$200,000+
  • White-label solutions – $55,000–$140,000

Custom features, security requirements, and third-party integrations significantly impact the final cost.

Q3. How Long Does It Take to Launch a Neobank Platform?

Launching a neobank platform can take anywhere from a few weeks to 6 months, depending on the level of customization, regulatory compliance, and technical complexity.

  • White-label solutions – 2–6 weeks
  • Custom-built platforms – 3–6 months

The timeline includes planning, UI/UX design, development, integrations, testing, and regulatory approvals.

Q4. What is Neobank?

Neobank is a type of digital-only banking that operates without any physical branches. It offers financial services through online apps giving users more convenience, lower fees, and robust features compared to traditional banks.

Q5. Is Neobank RBI approved?

Yes, Neobank operates under the regulatory framework of RBI, and businesses can also develop one similar to Neobank.

Q6. Are Neobanks Profitable?

Neobanks can be profitable, but it takes time. They often focus on growth first, earning revenue from premium services, transaction fees, lending, or partnerships. Large neobanks with millions of users usually reach profitability faster, while smaller ones may take years to break even.
